Captain Albert Clinton Myers, USN (Ret.)

United States Navy Surface Warfare Officer
Specialization: Anti-Submarine Warfare and Naval Operations

Overview

Captain Albert Clinton Myers, United States Navy (Retired), served as a career surface warfare officer with operational and command experience spanning the Cold War and post–Cold War period. His service included command at sea, squadron-level leadership, and contributions to naval strategic thought and anti-submarine warfare doctrine.

Education

  • B.S. Physics, Yale University (NROTC), 1970
  • M.A. National Security Affairs, Naval Postgraduate School, 1982
  • U.S. Army War College, 1985
  • Turkish Language Training, Defense Language Institute

Naval Career

Early Service

  • Communications Officer, USS Johnston (DD-821)
  • Operations Officer, USS Reeves (CG-24)
  • Operations/Readiness Officer, Destroyer Squadron 4
  • U.S. Defense Attaché Office, Ankara, Turkey (1975–1977)

Command and Senior Leadership

  • Executive Officer, USS Sample (FF-1048), 1982–1984
  • Commanding Officer, USS Sampson (DDG-10), 1988–1990
  • Commodore, Destroyer Squadron 22, 1994–1996

Operational Role

As Commodore of Destroyer Squadron 22, Captain Myers commanded a multinational naval force enforcing the United Nations embargo during the 1994 Haiti crisis in support of Operation Uphold Democracy.

Final Assignment

  • Military Assistant and Senior Operational Liaison to the Director, Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA)

Publications

  • “Khalkhin Gol: Stalin’s Battle to Stabilize the Soviet Far East,” Military Review, April 1983
  • “The Art of ASW,” U.S. Naval Institute Proceedings, October 1985 (with Captain Thomas M. Daly)

Decorations

  • Defense Superior Service Medal
  • Legion of Merit (three awards)

Career Timeline

  • 1970 – Commissioned via NROTC, Yale University
  • 1970s – Early sea and staff assignments, including USS Johnston and USS Reeves
  • 1975–1977 – Assignment in Ankara, Turkey
  • 1982–1984 – Executive Officer, USS Sample
  • 1985 – Graduate, U.S. Army War College
  • 1988–1990 – Commanding Officer, USS Sampson
  • 1994–1996 – Commodore, Destroyer Squadron 22
  • Final Tour – Military Assistant and Senior Operational Liaison to DARPA
  • 2000 – Retirement from the United States Navy
